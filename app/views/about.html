About The Practice

OFFICE VISITS, EMERGENCIES AND POLICIES

We take extra time during your first visit to familiarize ourselves with your medical history. Please arrive 5 minutes prior to the scheduled time of your first visit in order to complete a few forms. Please bring any medical records you feel may be helpful as well as a list of all your current health problems, past surgeries, medications you are taking and all allergies you may have experienced.

IF YOU ARE HAVING AN EMERGENCY CALL 911 FOR IMMEDIATE HELP
Appointment Cancellation or Rescheduling:

Please give at least 24 hours to cancel or reschedule an appointment. You may be charged for an appointment missed without prior notification. Patients who are more than 15 minutes late for an appointment will be rescheduled. If possible we will work you back into the schedule to be seen as soon as possible.

Office Hours:

Patients are seen in the office by appointment. Office hours are 8:30 AM to 5:00 PM, Monday through Friday. The answering system begins taking all calls after 5:00 PM.

Telephone Calls:

Education is an essential part of our responsibility to our patients and their families. You are welcome to call the office with questions during regular office hours. If we cannot speak with you immediately, someone will return your call as soon as possible. We try not to be interrupted when we are with you and request the same for other. Our doctors generally will return calls from the morning shortly after 12 noon and from the afternoon shortly after 5:00pm.

AFTER HOURS AND WEEKEND COVERAGE

If you need assistance after normal business hours, please call our main telephone number (352-331-4333) and follow the instructions provided. Please speak clearly and leave your contact telephone number. We ask that you repeat the number. Often when you are concerned you may speak too fast for us to write the number down correctly. Without the correct contact number we cannot call you back. The doctor on call will return your call as soon as possible. If you do not receive a return call within 30 minutes please call back as electronic devices do occasionally fail.

PAYMENT AND INSURANCE

  • Payment for medical services is due at the time the services are rendered. If you anticipate any difficulty with payment, please discuss this with our receptionist before your appointment. If notified in advance, we will consider special circumstances.
  • Please provide your current insurance card so we may confirm your coverage and copayments.
  • You will be asked for a photo I.D. which we can copy. This may be your drivers license or any other government issued I.D. We use this to assist in verifying your insurance as well as familiarizing our doctors and staff with identity should you call for prescriptions or other needs. We like to keep a face with each name!
  • We ask that you familiarize yourself with your basic insurance benefits and responsibilities, such as any co-payment, deductibles, or non-covered services which would be payable at the time of service.
  • We are providers for the following insurance companies: Aetna, Avmed, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Blue Options, Blue Choice, Cigna, First Health, Health Options, Humana, Tricare, United Healthcare.
  • Accepted forms of payment include cash, check, money order, VISA, MasterCard and Discover.

TRANSFER OF RECORDS

If you are entering our practice, please make arrangements with your former physician for the transfer of all essential medical information to this office. If you are leaving our practice, we extend the same courtesy to your new physician. To obtain a copy of your medical records, please complete the transfer of records form. The form can either be brought to the office or faxed to 352-331-8382

CONSULTATION AND REFERRAL

Most problems can be managed in the office or in one of the local hospitals. In some cases, however, problems requiring extremely sophisticated techniques or subspecialty care arise. Referral of such cases is generally made. Occasionally, you may want a second opinion about you concern from another physician. We encourage openness and honesty in this regard, and are quite ready to discuss such a desire with you.
